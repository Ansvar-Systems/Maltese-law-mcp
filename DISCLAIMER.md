## Data Authority and Limitations

### Source Authority

This Tool aggregates data from official Maltese legal databases. However:

| Source Type | Authority Level | Use Case |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **legislation.mt** | Authoritative | Statute text, consolidated legislation (primary reference) |
| **Government Gazette (Il-Gazzetta tal-Gvern)** | Authoritative | Legislation as enacted |
| **Community/open-data sources** | Supplementary | Cross-references, metadata (verify independently) |
| **EUR-Lex / treaty databases** | Authoritative for EU/international law | EU directives, regulations, international obligations |

**CRITICAL**: Community-maintained resources are **NOT** official government publications. While generally reliable, they may contain transcription errors, outdated information, or incomplete coverage.

### Bilingual Legal System

Malta operates a bilingual legal system. Many laws exist in both **Maltese (Malti)** and **English**. When a discrepancy exists between language versions:

- The **Government Gazette version** is authoritative
- Maltese is the national language; English has equal official status under the Constitution
- This Tool may not capture all bilingual nuances — verify the controlling language version for your specific matter

### Commercial Legal Databases

For professional legal work, **always cross-check with official Maltese legal resources**:

- legislation.mt (official, free), Malta Law Reports, Malta Law Journal

These resources provide editorial oversight and professional-grade currency guarantees that this Tool **cannot** provide.

## Currency and Staleness

### No Real-Time Updates

- Database updates are **manual** and may lag official publications by **weeks or months**
- Maltese law changes continuously through new Acts (Atti tal-Parlament), subsidiary legislation (leġiżlazzjoni sussidjarja), amendments, and court decisions (sentenzi / judgments)
- **Last-updated timestamps** in tool responses indicate data age, but should be treated as unreliable

### Staleness Warnings

The Tool includes staleness warnings when data is >30 days old. **These warnings are not exhaustive**:

- Even "fresh" data may be outdated if recent amendments were published in the Government Gazette (Il-Gazzetta tal-Gvern)
- Staleness warnings do not guarantee completeness or accuracy
- Always check official sources for the current legal position (pożizzjoni legali attwali)

### Amendments and Repeals

- A provision marked "in force" may have been:
  - Amended after the last database update
  - Repealed but not yet removed from the database
  - Subject to transitional provisions (dispożizzjonijiet tranżitorji) affecting its applicability
- The Tool does **NOT** automatically track amendments in real-time

## Coverage Gaps

### What's Missing

This Tool does **NOT** include:

1. **Complete case law** — Coverage of the Court of Appeal (Qorti tal-Appell), Civil Court, Criminal Court, and Constitutional Court decisions is limited; do not rely solely on this for case law research
2. **Historical statute versions** — Limited availability of historical provision wording
3. **Legal commentary** — No editorial annotations, academic commentary (duttrina legali), or practice guides
4. **Unpublished decisions** — Lower court decisions and administrative decisions largely absent
5. **Full legislative history** — Limited coverage of parliamentary materials (dibattiti parlamentari, bills, committee reports)

### Impact on Professional Use

These gaps mean:

- **Incomplete Legal Research**: Tool results are inherently incomplete and may miss critical authorities
- **Context Missing**: Without commentary (kummentarju) and legislative materials, interpretation may be incorrect
- **International Law Blind Spots**: Cannot fully assess EU law obligations or international treaty compliance
